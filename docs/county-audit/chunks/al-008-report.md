## Scope

This is the eighth Alabama county-audit chunk. It reviews the next `5` counties in the state checklist:

- Jackson

- Lawrence

## Findings

- `Jackson County` resolved to `complete-county-source-found`.
- `Jefferson`, `Lamar`, `Lauderdale`, and `Lawrence` currently resolve to `complete-state-source-only`.
- Jackson has two usable county-specific invasive signals in the reviewed public sources: the county resiliency plan flags invasive eel grass along the Tennessee River corridor, and Alabama Forestry Commission confirmed emerald ash borer in Jackson County in 2025.
- Lauderdale surfaced a county-specific wildlife disease notice during review, but it was chronic wasting disease rather than an invasive-species record, so it was explicitly excluded from invasive coverage decisions.

## Contradictions Preserved

- County-specific biological reporting is not automatically invasive-species evidence. Lauderdale County is the clearest example in this chunk because it had a county-specific wildlife disease notice that would have been misleading if treated as invasive coverage.
- Jackson is stronger than the other four counties in this tranche, but even there the source stack is still narrower than a broad county invasive-species inventory.
- The absence of county-specific invasive proof in Jefferson, Lamar, Lauderdale, and Lawrence does not mean those counties lack invasive species. It means the reviewed public source stack still did not expose county-level invasive reporting strong enough for production use.
