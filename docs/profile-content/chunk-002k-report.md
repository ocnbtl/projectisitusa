# Chunk 2K Report

## Scope

Chunk `2K` is a small mixed pass built around species with clearer public-action value than the thinnest remaining backup-pool holdouts. It adds `5` curated profiles and raises the curated total from `227` to `232`.

Profiles added in this batch:

- Asian Tiger Mosquito
- Argentine Ant
- Redbay Ambrosia Beetle
- Cane Toad
- Capybara

## Contradictions Resolved

The main contradiction after `2J` was whether to keep forcing the original backup pool even though several leftovers still had weaker public-facing source stacks than fresher candidates.

This batch resolves that explicitly:

- `Gilpinia hercyniae` and `Gekko gecko` were not promoted just to keep the old shortlist alive.
- The next pass stayed mixed, but it favored clearer official guidance over loyalty to the leftover pool.
- The workflow still followed the same source and chunk discipline instead of turning into opportunistic one-offs.

Another naming contradiction was addressed here:

- `Aedes albopictus` still surfaced as `Tiger mosquito` in registry data.
- The curated entry now uses `Asian Tiger Mosquito`, which is the clearer public-facing name and lines up with CDC usage.

## What Improved

- The batch strengthened the public-health and backyard-safety side of the catalog. `Asian Tiger Mosquito` and `Cane Toad` both have concrete household action paths instead of generic invasive-species language.
- The insect category gained more range in impact type. `Argentine Ant` is a household and garden systems problem, while `Redbay Ambrosia Beetle` is a forest and tree-mortality problem.
- `Capybara` keeps the wildlife category broad without overstating urgency. The profile is framed as a report-first nonnative mammal issue, which is more honest than pretending every sighting requires the same response as feral swine or iguanas.

## Weak Spots In This Batch

- `Capybara` remains a thinner source ecosystem than the strongest Florida reptile or fish profiles. The report-first tone is deliberate and should stay conservative.
- `Redbay Ambrosia Beetle` is necessarily tied to laurel wilt context, so the profile walks close to disease framing even though the entry is filed under insects.
- `Asian Tiger Mosquito` currently enters the curated set without a local species image, which is acceptable for now but still weaker than the image-backed profile standard.

## Process Lessons

- The backup pool should stay subordinate to source quality, not the other way around.
- Public-safety and homeowner-action species can remain distinct if the writing stays specific about the actual action path.
- A small chunk can still be worthwhile when it adds clearer naming, stronger public guidance, and better category breadth.

## Recommendation For The Next Batch

1. Continue treating the old backup pool as optional, not mandatory.
2. Prefer species with either strong official public guidance or a clearly fixable public-facing name problem.
3. Revisit deferred candidates only after their source stack improves enough to support distinct copy without filler.
